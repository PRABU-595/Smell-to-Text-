\section{Conclusion}

We presented NeoBERT-Smell, a transformer-based approach for translating natural language smell descriptions to chemical compound predictions. Our model significantly outperforms traditional baselines and demonstrates the potential of NLP techniques in the fragrance domain.

\subsection{Key Findings}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Transformer models can effectively learn smell-to-molecule mappings from textual descriptions.
    \item Data augmentation improves model robustness, especially for rare chemical compounds.
    \item Domain-specific tokenization provides marginal but consistent improvements.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Limitations}

Our approach has several limitations:
\begin{itemize}
    \item The model is limited to chemicals present in the training data.
    \item Subjective aspects of smell perception are not captured.
    \item Expert validation is limited in scale.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Future Work}

Future directions include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Incorporating molecular structure information alongside text.
    \item Expanding to predict quantitative concentrations.
    \item Developing an inverse model for chemical-to-smell description generation.
\end{itemize}
